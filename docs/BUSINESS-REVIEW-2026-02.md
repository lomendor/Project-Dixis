## Capital Reality Check (~€5K)

With ~€5,000 available capital and ~€150/mo fixed costs:

| Scenario | Monthly spend | Runway | Buffer at month 12 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zero marketing (theoretical) | €150/mo | ~33 months | €3,200 |
| Realistic (€150 fixed + €50-100 unexpected) | €200-250/mo | **20-24 months** | €1,500-2,000 |
| With commission revenue (€100/mo avg) | Net €100-150/mo | **30+ months** | **€3,000+** |

**Key insight**: €5K is ENOUGH because:
1. Fixed costs are only €150/mo (accountant). Everything else is already paid or variable.
2. In practice, expect €50-100/mo unexpected costs (returns, photos, small tools). Realistic burn: €200-250/mo.
3. Commission revenue starts reducing net burn from month 3-4.
4. **Break-even on fixed costs** = €200-250/mo commission = needs ~€2,000-2,500/mo GMV at ~10% avg rate.
5. That's roughly 50-65 orders/month × €40 AOV — achievable by month 6-8.

**What €5K does NOT allow**: Any paid marketing before commission revenue. This means organic-only (social, word of mouth, friends/family) for the first 3-6 months.

## What's NOT a problem

1. **12% commission** is fair for Greek food niche marketplace
2. **Technology is complete** — platform is LIVE, all systems working
3. **Competition** is Yoleni's/Farmers Republic/local markets, NOT Etsy
4. **First months will be slow** — this is NORMAL for any marketplace

---

## Competitor Context

- **Etsy**: 6.5% + fees, but international/general/English. NOT a direct competitor.
- **Yoleni's**: Premium Greek food, higher prices, physical stores. Different segment.
- **Farmers Republic**: Fresh produce, Attica only. Dixis is national.
- **Wikifarmer**: Global B2B, no B2C focus. Different market.
- **Supermarket e-shops**: Mass products, no artisan focus.

Dixis differentiator: **Greek-only, artisan food, D2C, national coverage, COD, Greek language, transparent commission.**
